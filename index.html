WIMLE: Uncertainty-Aware World Models with IMLE for Sample-Efficient Continuous Control

Mehran Aghabozorgi, Yanshu Zhang, Alireza Moazeni, Ke Li

WIMLE is a model-based reinforcement learning method that improves robustness of synthetic rollouts by combining IMLE-based multi-modal world modeling with transition-level uncertainty weighting. Across DeepMind Control, HumanoidBench, and MyoSuite, WIMLE improves sample efficiency and asymptotic performance on challenging continuous-control tasks.

Paper Code

Method Overview

WIMLE addresses common MBRL failure modes caused by model bias and compounding rollout errors:

  • IMLE world model: captures multi-modal transition dynamics instead of collapsing to unimodal predictions.
  • Dual uncertainty: aleatoric uncertainty from latent sampling and epistemic uncertainty from ensembles.
  • Uncertainty-weighted learning: all synthetic transitions are retained, but their influence is scaled by confidence.

Benchmarks

  • DeepMind Control Suite (including hard Dog/Humanoid tasks)
  • HumanoidBench
  • MyoSuite
