WIMLE: Uncertainty-Aware World Models with IMLE for Sample-Efficient Continuous Control

WIMLE is a model-based RL framework that combines IMLE-based multi-modal world models with uncertainty-aware weighting, improving both sample efficiency and final performance on challenging continuous-control benchmarks.

What is WIMLE?

Model-based RL can be highly sample efficient, but often suffers from rollout bias and compounding model errors. WIMLE addresses this with three ingredients: (1) a stochastic, multi-modal world model trained with IMLE, (2) dual uncertainty estimation (aleatoric + epistemic), and (3) uncertainty-weighted learning that reduces the influence of unreliable synthetic transitions without discarding useful long-horizon information.

  • Multi-modal dynamics modeling: IMLE captures diverse outcomes for the same state-action pair.
  • Confidence-aware training: synthetic targets are reweighted by transition-level uncertainty.
  • Stronger performance: substantial gains in sample efficiency and asymptotic return.
